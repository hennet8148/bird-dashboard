About the Project

Bird Monitor is a handcrafted acoustic monitoring project that uses BirdNET and Raspberry Pi to continuously identify bird species by sound. It’s deployed on a 100-acre family farm in northeastern Pennsylvania, surrounded by a diverse mix of forest, field, and wetland habitats.

The goal is to create a long-term, high-resolution record of avian presence and activity — not just for scientific curiosity, but to connect more deeply with the land, understand changes in biodiversity, and build something meaningful, local, and open.
